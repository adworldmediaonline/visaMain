## Is it necessary for US citizens to obtain a visa for Sri Lanka?

Yes, US citizens are required to obtain a visa for Sri Lanka. However, they fall under the category of eligible nationalities, allowing them to apply for the Sri Lanka visa online, which offers a more convenient option compared to visiting the Sri Lankan embassy in person.

## Sri Lanka Visa Requirements for US Citizens

Here are the essentials for US citizens applying for a Sri Lanka visa:

-   Ensure you have a valid passport.
-   Complete the application form accurately.
-   Pay the visa fees using a bank card.
-   Provide an active email address to receive the approved travel documentation.

## Application Process

-   **Online Application:** The Sri Lanka visa for U.S. citizens can be conveniently applied for online, making it more comfortable than visiting the Sri Lankan embassy in person.
-   **ETA or Visa:** Choose between an ETA (Electronic Travel Authorization) or a traditional visa. The ETA allows for a 30-day visa-free period and permits multiple entries within 30 days from your first arrival in Sri Lanka.

## Cost of Sri Lanka Visa for US Citizens

The Sri Lanka visa fee for US citizens is 40 USD, payable using a credit or debit card.

## Processing Time for Sri Lanka Visa

The processing time typically falls within the range of 3 to 4 days. To account for any unforeseen issues, it is advisable to apply at least 15 days before your travel date, but no more than 90 days in advance.

## Vaccine Requirements for Sri Lanka Visa for US Citizens

US citizens visiting Sri Lanka are not subject to mandatory vaccine requirements. However, if a US citizen has recently traveled to a country endemic for yellow fever or had a layover in such a country for more than 12 hours, they must present a yellow fever vaccination certificate to gain access to Sri Lanka.

While not mandatory, the CDC recommends various vaccinations for all travelers entering Sri Lanka, including Chickenpox, Diphtheria-Tetanus-Pertussis, Flu, Measles-Mumps-Rubella (MMR), Polio, Shingles, Hepatitis A and B, Japanese encephalitis, Rabies, and Typhoid.
