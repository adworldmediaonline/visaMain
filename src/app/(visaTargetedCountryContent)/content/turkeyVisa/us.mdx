### Best Things to Do In Turkey with Turkish eVisa

Turkey is a wonderful destination for US citizens who want to experience its rich history, culture, and nature. As a US citizen, you can visit Turkey visa-free for up to 90 days within every 180 days. However, you will need a valid passport with at least six months of validity remaining and one blank page for entry and exit stamps.

Here are some interesting things to do in Turkey:

1. **Pamukkale Water Terraces**: Explore the Pamukkale Water Terraces, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that features stunning white travertine pools and hot springs.

2. **Basilica Cisterns of Istanbul**: Visit the Basilica Cisterns of Istanbul, an ancient underground reservoir that was built by the Byzantine Empire and featured in the James Bond movie From Russia with Love.

3. **Lycian Rock Tombs**: Marvel at the Lycian Rock Tombs, ancient tombs carved into the cliffs overlooking the Mediterranean Sea in Fethiye.

4. **Myra Necropolis**: Discover the Myra Necropolis, a complex of rock-cut tombs and a Roman theater in Demre, the birthplace of Saint Nicholas.

5. **Cemberlitas Hamami**: Enjoy a Turkish bath at Cemberlitas Hamami, one of the oldest and most famous hamams (traditional bathhouses) in Istanbul.

6. **Troy**: Experience the archaeological site of Troy if you’ve watched the movie TROY (:D), the legendary city of the Trojan War and the wooden horse.

7. **Pamukkale Thermal Pools**: Take a natural sauna at the Pamukkale thermal pools, where you can relax in the mineral-rich waters and enjoy the views of the surrounding landscape.

8. **Goreme National Park**: Explore the limestone formations and fairy chimneys at Goreme National Park, another UNESCO World Heritage Site that is home to cave churches, monasteries, and underground cities.

9. **Gobekli Tepe**: Visit the Gobekli Tepe, the oldest known temple in the world, dating back to 10,000 BC.

These are just some of the many attractions and activities that Turkey has to offer.
