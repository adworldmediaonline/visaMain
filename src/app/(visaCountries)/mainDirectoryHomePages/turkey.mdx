## Important Landmarks and Attractions

### Hagia Sophia:

An ancient Istanbul structure that has served as a mosque and a church is known as Hagia Sophia. Notable interior features include mosaics and marble pillars, and the enormous dome is the building's most recognisable feature.

### Blue Mosque:

Also located in Istanbul is the Blue Mosque, which is famous for the beautiful blue tiles that adorn the inside walls of the building. It is still used for prayer and features six towers called minarets.

### Topkapi Palace:

The former residence of the sultans of Turkey is Topkapi Palace. The palace is quite spacious, featuring numerous apartments and picturesque courtyards. There are currently artefacts and clothing on display in the museum.

### Cappadocia:

The peculiar rock formations in cappadocia are known as "fairy chimneys." Homes, churches, and even entire towns have been sculpted out of these pliable rocks by humans. To get a bird's-eye view of the
breathtaking scenery, you may also take a balloon flight here.

### Antalya:

Situated on the Mediterranean Sea coast, this city is well-known for its beautiful beaches. You can swim, sunbathe or simply unwind here.

### Pamukkale:

This place is well-known for its travertine terraces, which give the impression of snowy slopes. For countless generations, people have taken use of the natural pools of warm water formed by these terraces. View the terraces and the countryside below in all their glory from above on a hot air balloon flight.

## Best time to Visit Turkey

Turkey is at its most picturesque in the spring (April–June) and fall (September–November), when visitors can enjoy pleasant weather perfect for sightseeing. Coastal areas often experience scorching summers, whereas inland regions often see snowfall in winter.
