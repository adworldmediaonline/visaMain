## Borra Caves, Visakhapatnam

The Borra Caves are one of the largest and deepest cave systems in India, located in the Eastern Ghats of Andhra Pradesh. They are famous for their spectacular stalactites and stalagmites formations, which resemble various shapes and figures. The caves also have a religious significance, as they house a Shiva temple and a natural lingam. The Borra Caves are a natural wonder that will leave you spellbound.

## Mysore Palace, Mysore

The Mysore Palace is one of the most magnificent and grandiose palaces in India, located in the city of Mysore in Karnataka. It was the official residence of the Wodeyar dynasty, who ruled Mysore for over six centuries. The palace is a blend of various architectural styles, such as Indo-Saracenic, Rajput, Gothic, and Islamic. The palace is adorned with intricate carvings, paintings, domes, arches, and pillars. The palace also has a museum that displays the royal artefacts, weapons, costumes, and paintings. The Mysore Palace is a must-see for anyone who loves history and art.

## Bhimbetka Rock Shelters, Raisen

The Bhimbetka Rock Shelters are a UNESCO World Heritage Site, located in the Raisen district of Madhya Pradesh. They are a collection of over 700 rock shelters, dating back to the Paleolithic and Mesolithic periods. They contain some of the oldest and finest rock paintings in the world, depicting the life and culture of the ancient humans. The paintings depict animals, hunting, dancing, rituals, and warfare. The Bhimbetka Rock Shelters are a testimony to the evolution of human civilization and creativity.

## Lingaraja Temple Complex, Khurda

The Lingaraja Temple Complex is one of the oldest and largest temple complexes in India, located in the city of Bhubaneswar in Odisha. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva, who is worshipped as Lingaraja, the king of the lingas. The temple complex consists of over 150 temples and shrines, built in the Kalinga style of architecture. The main temple is a marvel of stone carving, with a 55-metre-high tower, a spacious hall, and a sanctum. The temple complex also has a large water tank, where devotees take a holy dip.

## Jallianwala Bagh, Amritsar

The Jallianwala Bagh is a historical and memorial site, located in the city of Amritsar in Punjab. It is the site of a tragic massacre that took place on April 13, 1919, when British troops fired upon a peaceful gathering of unarmed Indians, killing hundreds of them. The site has a bullet-riddled wall, a well, and a garden, where the victims are commemorated. The site also has a museum that displays the photographs, documents, and artefacts related to the massacre. The Jallianwala Bagh is a place of sorrow and courage, where you can pay your respects to the martyrs of the freedom struggle.

## Ghats and Old City of Pushkar, Pushkar

The Ghats and Old City of Pushkar are a religious and cultural hub, located in the town of Pushkar in Rajasthan. Pushkar is one of the oldest and holiest cities in India, known for its sacred lake, the Pushkar Lake, which has 52 ghats or steps leading to the water. The lake is believed to have been created by Lord Brahma, the creator of the universe, and is surrounded by hundreds of temples and ashrams. The old city of Pushkar is a maze of narrow lanes, colourful bazaars, and quaint cafes. Pushkar is also famous for its annual camel fair, which attracts thousands of tourists and pilgrims.

## Fatehpur Sikri, Agra

The Fatehpur Sikri is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, located in the city of Agra in Uttar Pradesh. It was the capital of the Mughal Empire from 1571 to 1585, built by Emperor Akbar. It is a masterpiece of Indo-Islamic architecture, with a blend of Persian, Turkish, and Indian elements. The complex consists of palaces, mosques, tombs, gardens, and courtyards, built with red sandstone and marble. Some of the notable structures are the Buland Darwaza, the Jama Masjid, the Panch Mahal, the Diwan-i-Khas, and the Tomb of Salim Chishti. The Fatehpur Sikri is a magnificent and majestic site that will transport you to the glory of the Mughal era.

## Jantar Mantar Observatory, Jaipur

The Jantar Mantar Observatory is a scientific and astronomical wonder, located in the city of Jaipur in Rajasthan. It was built by Maharaja Jai Singh II in the 18th century, as a collection of 19 instruments for measuring time, predicting eclipses, tracking stars, and calculating celestial movements. The instruments are made of stone and marble, and are designed in the form of geometric shapes and structures. The largest and most impressive instrument is the Samrat Yantra, a giant sundial that can tell the time with an accuracy of two seconds.

## Agra Fort, Uttar Pradesh

The Agra Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, located in the city of Agra in Uttar Pradesh. It was the main residence of the Mughal emperors from 1556 to 1638, built by Emperor Akbar. It is a massive and majestic fort, with a circumference of 2.5 kilometres and a height of 21 metres. It is made of red sandstone and white marble, and has four gates, two of which are open to the public. The fort contains several palaces, mosques, halls, gardens, and towers, each with its own history and beauty. Some of the notable structures are the Delhi Gate, the Lahore Gate, the Diwan-i-Aam, the Diwan-i-Khas, the Sheesh Mahal, and the Musamman Burj. The Agra Fort is a splendid and spectacular site that will mesmerise you with its grandeur and elegance.

## Junagarh Fort, Bikaner

This is a splendid and imposing fort, built by the Rathore rulers of Bikaner in the 16th century. The fort is surrounded by a moat, and has several gates, courtyards, and palaces, such as the Suraj Pol, the Karan Mahal, the Anup Mahal, and the Badal Mahal. The fort also has a museum, which displays weapons, armors, paintings, and royal costumes.
