## So, What's Needed to Visit India? 

To visit India, people from Sydney, Australia need to apply for an e-Visa, which is an online authorization that allows them to enter and stay in India for a limited period and purpose. 

The e-Visa process is simple and convenient, and involves the following steps**:**

**Step-1:** Apply online by filling out a form and uploading a photo and a passport page.

**Step-2:** Pay the e-Visa fee online using a credit or debit card or a payment wallet.

**Step-3:** Receive the Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) by email, which is valid for one year from the date of issue.

**Step-4:** Print the ETA and present it at the immigration check post in India, where the e-Visa will be stamped on the passport.

The e-Visa is available for the following categories**:** 

- e-Tourist Visa

### Which are some popular festivals in India that Australians can enjoy?

India is a country of vibrant festivals that offer a unique glimpse into its rich culture and traditions. Here are some popular Indian festivals that Australians might enjoy**:**

**Holi:** Often referred to as the ‚ÄúFestival of Colors‚Äù, Holi is one of the best-known festivals outside of India1. The festival involves people throwing coloured powder on each other and squirting each other with water guns.

**Diwali:** Also known as the ‚ÄúFestival of Lights‚Äù, Diwali is one of the most significant festivals in India. It celebrates the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

**Pongal:** This is a four-day harvest festival celebrated in honour of the sun god, Surya2. It marks the beginning of the northward journey of the sun and is usually celebrated around January every year.

**Navratri/Durga Puja:** This is a nine-night festival that honours the goddess Durga. It involves fasting, dancing, and culminates in the immersion of Durga idols in water.

**Raksha Bandhan:** This festival celebrates the bond between brothers and sisters. Sisters tie a protective thread around their brothers‚Äô wrists, and in return, brothers give gifts.

**Eid-Ul-Fitr:** This festival marks the end of Ramadan, the Islamic holy month of fasting. It involves communal prayers, feasting, and giving of gifts.

**Ganesh Chaturthi:** This festival celebrates the birth of Lord Ganesha, the elephant-headed god of wisdom and prosperity. It involves creating clay idols of Ganesha, offering prayers, and immersing the idols in water.

**Onam:** This is a harvest festival celebrated in the state of Kerala. It involves boat races, tiger dances, elaborate feasts, and the creation of flower rangolis.

**Dussehra:** This festival celebrates the victory of Lord Rama over the demon king Ravana. It involves the burning of effigies of Ravana and culminates in the festival of Diwali.

**Maha Shivratri:** This festival is dedicated to Lord Shiva. Devotees fast and offer prayers to Shiva throughout the night.

**Remember:** The dates of these festivals can change each year based on lunar calendars, so it‚Äôs always a good idea to check the dates before planning your trip.
